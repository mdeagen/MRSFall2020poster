Authoring Interactive and Extensible Visualizations from a Materials Knowledge Graph

Michael Deagen, Ph.D. | University of Vermont

F.MT07.10.05

Michael.Deagen@uvm.edu

My name is Michael Deagen, here to give you an overview of the new dataviz platform on our open materials science knowledge graph, MaterialsMine.

Knowledge graphs enable a web of data

A knowledge graph database stores data and the links between data in a machine-readable format. By following semantic web standards, we enable interoperability between MaterialsMine and other open data resources.

Objectives

  • Explore datasets linked by knowledge graph
  • Share information visually and interactively
  • Build collection of FAIR data charts
Our new data visualization platform helps us to explore linked datasets, share information from our knowledge graph interactively, and build a collection of charts that take advantage of our FAIR data framework.

How would you prepare a meal from scratch?

Market + List + Ingredients + Recipe = Meal

Imagine you’re preparing a meal from scratch. You go to the market with your grocery list and add ingredients to your cart. After you follow the steps laid out in the recipe, you finally get to the enjoy the product of your hard work.

Most charts provide limited or no context for data

The problem with many charts out there is that they provide the view of the data without embedding the raw data or how those data were encoded visually.

Our charts are defined by a data query and chart spec

Database + Query + Dataset + Spec = Chart

With our platform, you can think of the market as our database. The grocery list is the query, or request from the database. The groceries in your cart comprise the dataset returned by that query, and the chart spec is the recipe that defines how the data are visually encoded. These combine to form the final chart view of the data.

Benefits

  • Embed context of data
  • Charts with interactive elements
  • Real-time updating of chart data
This approach to dataviz gives context to the data, allows charts to be specified with interactive elements, and by embedding the query a chart can be automatically refreshed as new data arrive.

Combining SPARQL query language with Vega-Lite chart specification language lets us define custom, interactive charts with minimal lines of code

The query, written in SPARQL, and the chart spec, written in Vega-Lite, are high-level abstractions that let you build custom charts with minimal lines of code while the low-level implementation details are managed under the hood. Just as there are many ways to prepare food from the same ingredients, there are many ways to specify a chart from a given dataset.

Chart metadata (query, spec, title, description) added to knowledge graph, findable in Charts Gallery

When charts are published to our platform, their metadata become part of the knowledge graph and can be found in our Charts Gallery. As a result, the knowledge graph continues to grow as users create useful views of datasets.
